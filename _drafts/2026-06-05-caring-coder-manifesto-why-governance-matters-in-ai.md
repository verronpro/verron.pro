In the rush to integrate AI into everything, we're losing sight of **Craftsmanship**. For me, being a "Caring Coder" means more than just passing tests; it's about the governance we build around our tools.

### Craftsmanship as Risk Management
In high-stakes sectors, a "good enough" automation isn't good enough. If a document generator produces a faulty safety report because an LLM was unconstrained, the "coder" is responsible. 

### Why I Keep Caring
I'll keep caring about document generation—even as AI gets better—because the *handover* between human intent and machine execution is where the most risk lives. By applying strict Java standards and security-first SpEL patterns, we're not just building a library; we're building a safety net for automated decisions.
